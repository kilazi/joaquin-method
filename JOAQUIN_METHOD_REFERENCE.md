## 1. Method Architecture

The method has three distinct layers. Confusing them is the most common mistake.

> *"Modelo de lectura, modelo de ataque, y todo eso delineado con gestion. Punto."*
> ("Reading model, attack model, and all that outlined with management. Period.")

### Layer 1: Reading Model (Modelo de Lectura)
**What it does:** Positions you on the chart. Tells you WHERE price is likely to go.
**Components:** Cycles, fractality, zones, Points of Control, context reading.
**This is what the free YouTube course teaches.** It is NOT a strategy by itself.

### Layer 2: Attack Model (Modelo de Ataque)
**What it does:** Tells you HOW to enter trades based on the reading.
**Components:** Zone confluences, liquidity grabs, entry patterns (Pauta 1/Pauta 2), Fibonacci level entries.
**This is personal.** There are "a thousand ways to attack price." Liquidity grabs alone can be a complete attack model.

### Layer 3: Management (Gestion)
**What it does:** Controls risk, stops, partials, position sizing, exits.
**Components:** Business plan, trade classification (5 types), partial strategies, super-winner psychology, the 10% floating profit rule.
**This is deeply individual.** No two students trade the same way.

### How They Connect
```
Reading Model → "Price should go UP to these levels"
     ↓
Attack Model → "Enter HERE via liquidity grab / zone confluence / pattern"
     ↓
Management  → "Risk $X, partial at Y, stop at Z, target at W"
     ↓
Complete Method = Reading + Attack + Management
```

> *"Lo que nunca deberia tener es una estrategia para atacar el precio si no tengo una localizacion en el mapa."*
> ("What I should never have is a strategy to attack price without having a location on the map.")

## 2. Core Concepts

### 2.1 Point of Control (Punto de Control / PoC)

**Definition:** The origin of a significant price movement. A swing high or swing low from which a measurable impulse launched.

**Fractal Identification Protocol:**
1. Identify a fractal: an impulse movement leaving a peak with **at least 2 closed candles to the left AND 2 closed candles to the right** of the peak.
2. This fractal is your candidate Point of Control.

> *"Un fractal es un movimiento impulsivo que debe dejar un pico. Debe tener una secuencia de dos velas a la izquierda, dos velas a la derecha."*

**The 1/3 Validation Rule (Regla del Tercio):**
A PoC is NOT valid until price surpasses the fractal high by at least **1/3 of the retracement height**.

**Step-by-step:**
1. Measure the retracement height in pips (from fractal high to retracement low).
2. Divide by 3.
3. Price must exceed the fractal high by at least that value.

**Example:** Retracement = 122 pips. 122 / 3 = ~40 pips. Price must exceed the fractal high by at least 40 pips. If it only exceeds by 26 pips — **FALSE BREAKOUT. Manipulation.**

> *"Para que yo diga que tengo un punto de control correcto, necesito que el precio supere el alto del fractal en al menos un tercio de la altura del retroceso."*

> *"Superas 40 pips ese nivel? 26! No! Falso rompimiento. Eso es manipulacion."*

**PoC Obsolescence Rule:** When a new retracement is LARGER than all previous retracements AND it validates via the 1/3 rule, ALL previous Points of Control die simultaneously. Minor fractals give way to major fractals.

> *"Los fractales menores conducen a fractales mayores. Los fractales menores se vuelven obsoletos, dando paso a fractales mayores."*

---

### 2.2 Fractality (Fractalidad)

**Definition:** The self-similar, evolving nature of price movements across all timeframes. Minor fractals lead to major fractals. This is the core principle.

**Key Rules:**
- Every cycle WILL either **break** or **evolve**. This is absolute certainty.
- Minor fractals become obsolete when major fractals form.
- The concept is identical on all timeframes.
- Fractality must be "coherent" and "typical" for the method to work well. When an asset becomes "random" and "lacks fractality," the method struggles.

> *"Todos los fractales que te dan un ciclo, todos, o se rompen o evolucionan."*
> ("All the fractals that give you a cycle, all of them, either break or evolve.")

**How Minor Fractals Lead to Major Fractals:**
1. A small impulse creates a small cycle (minor fractal).
2. Price respects the zones and reverses.
3. The reversal creates a LARGER movement than the original impulse.
4. This larger movement retraces to 38.2%.
5. The new, larger cycle (major fractal) replaces the old one entirely.
6. All old zones, levels, and marks are **dead**.

> *"La fractalidad menor se esta rompiendo porque se estan formando fractales mayores. No me quiero quedar con un fractal menor. Quiero trabajar las zonas del fractal mayor."*

### 2.3 Cycles (Ciclos)

**Definition:** The fundamental unit of the reading model. A cycle is created when an impulse from a Point of Control is followed by a retracement to 38.2%.

**Cycle Activation Conditions:**
1. Valid Point of Control exists (validated via 1/3 rule).
2. Impulse movement from that PoC.
3. Retracement to the **38.2% Fibonacci level** of the impulse.
4. **Cycle is now ACTIVATED.** Mark all zones.

> *"El retroceso es la condicion imprescindible para que definamos que efectivamente cuando hay retroceso de ese impulso al 38.2, ahi es el momento en el que podemos marcar todas sus zonas."*

**What to Mark When a Cycle Activates:**
- High Zone (Zona Alta) — above 61.8% of the impulse
- Low Zone (Zona Baja) — below the retracement area
- The 38.2 level (38 level) — mid-reference
- The 61.8 zone (Zona de 61) — primary working zone
- Break level of the cycle
- Direction arrows

**Direction Rules:**
- **Downtrend cycle:** Sell in high zones (61 downwards). Buy in low zones.
- **Uptrend cycle:** Buy in low zones (61 upwards). Sell in high zones.

> *"Siempre vender en zonas altas, en este caso bajista, 61 hacia abajo, y en este caso alcista, 61 hacia arriba y siempre comprar en las zonas bajas."*

### 2.4 Zones (Zonas)

| Zone | Spanish | Description | Action |
|------|---------|-------------|--------|
| **High Zone** | Zona Alta | Above 61.8% of the impulse | Sell attempts (in downtrend); Danger zone (in uptrend) |
| **Low Zone** | Zona Baja | Below the retracement area | Buy attempts (in uptrend); Danger zone (in downtrend) |
| **61.8 Zone** | Zona del 61 | The 61.8% Fibonacci level area | Primary working zone. Does NOT trigger evolution. |
| **38 Level** | Nivel del 38 | The 38.2% retracement level | Mid-reference point. Cycle activation trigger. |
| **161.8 Level** | Nivel 161 | Fibonacci extension level | First projection target after failure activation |
| **200 Level** | Nivel 200 | Fibonacci extension level | Extended target, especially for indices/commodities |

**Critical Zone Rule:** The moment price enters a High Zone, all other zones of that cycle (61, 38, Low Zone) are IMMEDIATELY discarded. Only the High Zone and its break level remain valid. Vice versa for Low Zone entry.

> *"Si en algun momento el precio entra en zona alta, esta zona de 61 es obvio que no vale, ese 38 es obvio que tampoco vale y esta zona de abajo es obvio que tampoco vale."*

**Zone Confluence:** When zones from different cycles overlap (e.g., Low Zone of one cycle + 38 level of another), this is a **mandatory trade** ("si o si").

> *"Esta coincidencia de la zona baja con el 38 azul es ya un motivo si o si de compra."*

### 2.5 Break vs. Evolution (Rotura vs. Evolucion)

Every cycle WILL either break or evolve. There are no other options.

#### Break (Rotura)
- Price exceeds the break level by **>= 1/3 of the retracement height** (same 1/3 rule).
- The cycle is **DEAD.** It will never serve again.
- Start looking for new Points of Control from the breakout movement.
- Anything less than 1/3 = **false breakout / manipulation.**

> *"Ese ciclo no vale, murio, se acabo, se rompio y ya nunca mas va a servir."*
> *"Para que haya una rotura valida, el precio debe superar el nivel en al menos un tercio de la altura del retroceso. Todo lo demas es manipulacion."*

**Trend Break Rule:** A trend is ONLY broken when the **LAST control cycle** in the trend direction breaks. Breaking intermediate cycles does NOT break the trend.

> *"La tendencia solo se rompe cuando el ultimo ciclo de control alcista se rompe."*

#### Evolution (Evolucion)
- Price respects a zone and reverses.
- The resulting movement is **LARGER** than the original impulse.
- This larger movement retraces to 38.2%.
- New cycle replaces the old one. All old zones are dead.
- This is how minor fractals give way to major fractals.

**When 61.8 is reached:** The 61.8 zone does NOT trigger evolution. The retracement is still smaller than the impulse. Trade normally.

> *"El 61.8 no afecta al ciclo. Podemos trabajarlo absolutamente pero no afecta a ninguna evolucion."*
> *"Cuando este movimiento viene a trabajar 61.8, el retroceso sigue siendo menor que el impulso. Por tanto, no hay problema."*

### 2.6 The 130% Rule (Regla del 130)

**Definition:** A retracement is NOT considered complete until price touches the **130% Fibonacci level** measured from the retracement.

**What 130% means:** Price must fall below (or rise above) the start of the retracement by **1/3 of the retracement's measure.**

> *"Mientras el precio no haga esto y toque el 130, yo no puedo decir que ese retroceso ha concluido."*
> *"Lo que pedimos es que caiga por debajo del inicio del retroceso un tercio de su medida."*

**Key behaviors:**
- If the retracement deepens (makes new highs/lows), **REMEASURE** the Fibonacci from the new extent. The 130 level moves.
- Retracements can be **fast** (quickly reach 130) or **slow** (take a long time, creating confusion).
- Until 130 is touched, you CANNOT confirm the next impulse has begun.

> *"Cojo mi Fibo y lo vuelvo a medir completamente. Desde donde empezo el retroceso, sigo midiendo con mi Fibo. Y fijate como el 130 cambia de sitio."*

---

### 2.7 The 1/3 Rule (Regla del Tercio)

This rule appears in THREE critical places:

| Application | What it validates | How |
|------------|-------------------|-----|
| **PoC Validation** | Is this a real Point of Control? | Price must surpass fractal high by >= 1/3 of retracement height |
| **Break Confirmation** | Has a cycle truly broken? | Price must exceed break level by >= 1/3 of retracement height |
| **PoC Obsolescence** | Are old PoCs dead? | New retracement must be larger AND validate with 1/3 rule |

**Rationale — The Institutional Counterpart:** Market makers manipulate price to trigger stops just below the 1/3 threshold. Only moves exceeding 1/3 represent genuine institutional commitment. Everything below = manipulation designed to trap retail traders.

### 2.8 Failure (Fracaso)

**Definition:** A price movement whose origin did not reach the **25% minimum** — a "defective origin" (origen defectivo / extremo defectivo).

> *"Esto es un origen de movimiento correcto? No. Por que no? Porque no ha llegado al 25%."*

**When a failure is activated:**
- Price has a projection to reach **161.8** or **200** level.
- Even if you're in a selling zone, you cannot ignore the long projection from a failure.
- Any **61.8 after failure of highs** is a reason to sell. Any **61.8 after failure of lows** is a reason to buy.

> *"Tengo una proyeccion para ir arriba. Tengo una proyeccion para ir al 200 o al 161.8."*
> *"A partir de aqui ya cualquier 61.8, esto ya era motivo de venta."*

**Asset-class difference for failure targets:**
- **Indices/Commodities:** Favor the **200 level** as target. 161.8 fails more often.
- **Currencies:** 161.8 is a valid first target. 200 as secondary.

> *"El 161 trabaja mucho mejor en divisas, en indices y materias primas falla bastante. Los 200 estan trabajando mejor."*

**Failure-Based Progression to 200:**
When the distance between 161.8 and 200 is large (high timeframe), price typically does NOT go straight to 200. Instead:
1. Price hits 161.8, fails
2. Retraces
3. Hits a new 161.8, fails again
4. Progressively works toward 200 through repeated failures

> *"Normalmente, para llegar ahi, lo hara normalmente asi: fracasos."*

### 2.9 Liquidity Grabs (Capturas de Liquidez)

**Definition:** Institutional manipulation where price breaks lows (or highs) to trigger stop-loss orders and accumulate positions before reversing.

**How to use them:**
- If your reading model says price should go UP: look for liquidity grabs that **break low points.**
- If your reading model says price should go DOWN: look for liquidity grabs that **break high points.**
- Liquidity grabs alone can be a complete trading strategy.

> *"Sitios de liquidez, en este caso, que rompan puntos bajos, porque mi modelo me dice que el precio tiene que subir mas."*
> *"Solo con esto puedes tener una metodologia, una estrategia, una estrategia de trabajo. Solo con esto."*
> *"No hace falta ningun puto ciclo porque los ciclos ya te han posicionado."*

## 3. Entry Decision Framework

### Step-by-Step Checklist: "Should I Enter This Trade?"

#### Step 1: Business Plan Check
- [ ] Do I have a defined max loss per trade?
- [ ] Do I have lot sizing rules?
- [ ] Am I within my risk parameters?
- [ ] Are all 5 profitability factors aligned (discipline, numbers, method, assets, personal condition)?

**If NO to any:** Do NOT trade.

#### Step 2: Context Check (Left Side of Chart)
- [ ] Have I analyzed the left side of the chart?
- [ ] What failures are activated?
- [ ] What liquidity has been left behind?
- [ ] Where are the historical Points of Control?
- [ ] Are movement origins correct (reached 25%) or defective?

> *"Nos falta contexto. No porque este en zona de venta asumimos que es para vender. No funciona asi."*

#### Step 3: Cycle Identification
- [ ] Is there a validated Point of Control (1/3 rule)?
- [ ] Has there been an impulse from it?
- [ ] Has the impulse retraced to 38.2%?
- [ ] Is the cycle ACTIVATED with all zones marked?
- [ ] Have any cycles evolved or broken recently?

#### Step 4: Zone Identification
- [ ] What zone is price currently in?
- [ ] Is it a High Zone, Low Zone, 61.8 zone, or between zones?
- [ ] Have I discarded obsolete zones (per the zone-entry discard rule)?

#### Step 5: Directional Bias
- [ ] What does the reading model say about direction?
- [ ] Are there failure projections active (161.8/200)?
- [ ] Is the cycle uptrend or downtrend?
- [ ] Do higher timeframe cycles agree with lower timeframe?

#### Step 6: Confluence Check
- [ ] Do multiple levels/zones agree at this price?
- [ ] Is there a zone confluence (e.g., Low Zone + 38 of another cycle)?
- [ ] Is a Fibonacci level coinciding with a cycle zone?
- [ ] Is there a 61.8 level after confirmed failure of highs/lows?

**Confluence = high conviction = "si o si" trade.**

#### Step 7: Entry Trigger
Choose ONE:
- **Zone Confluence** — Multiple zones/levels converge = mandatory entry
- **Liquidity Grab** — Price breaks lows (long bias) or highs (short bias) = enter on reversal
- **61.8 After Failure** — Any 61.8 retracement after failure of highs/lows = valid entry
- **Fibonacci Level + Bias** — Price at 38.2, 61.8, 161.8, or 200 aligned with directional bias

#### Step 8: Entry Execution
- **Single entry:** Full position, stop per business plan
- **Fractional entry:** 2 parts recommended. First at zone, second at deeper level. 3+ parts gets confusing.

> *"La gente que mejor trabaja con entrada fraccionada lo hace con dos. A partir de tres se hace confusa."*

#### Step 9: Entry Timing Acceptance
Accept that some entries will fail. This is deliberate. You sacrifice accuracy on minor setups to be perfectly positioned for significant zones.

> *"El timing de entrada implica sacrificar entradas fallidas para lograr un timing perfecto en las zonas significativas."*

## 4. Trade Management Rules

### 4.1 The 5 Trade Outcome Types

| Type | Spanish | What Happened | Example |
|------|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Complete Loser** | Operacion completamente perdedora | Price went straight to stop. No management possible. | Plan max loss = $50. Lost $50. |
| **Null Trade** | Operacion nula | Cut loss early when price showed no reversal intent. | Should have lost $50, cut at $25. |
| **Positive Trade** | Operacion positiva | Reached partial but then reversed. Small profit. | Made $40 but target was $300. |
| **Winning Trade** | Operacion ganadora | Met business plan profit target via partials/exit. | Target $300, made $300. |
| **Very Winning Trade** | Operacion muy ganadora | Exceeded target significantly. | Target $300, made $500+. |

**Critical insight:** Positive trades will NEVER grow your account. They only offset losses. Only winning and very winning trades grow the account.

> *"Una operacion positiva no hara nunca crecer tu cuenta, sirven para recuperar una operacion completamente perdedora o nula."*
> *"Estas operaciones positivas nunca van a hacer crecer tu cuenta."*

### 4.2 Fixed Ratio Management (Recommended to Master FIRST)

**Most common comfortable ratio: 1:4**

**Version A (No Partials):**
1. Enter trade.
2. When price reaches **1:1 risk-reward** — move stop to **HALF** the original distance.
3. Target remains at **1:4**.
4. Management is done. No further adjustments.

> *"Cuando el precio esta en el 1 a 1, colocan el stop a la mitad. Se acabo la gestion."*

**Version B (With Partials):**
1. Enter trade.
2. When price reaches **1:1** — take **HALF** the position off (partial). Move stop to HALF.
3. Remaining position targets extended level.
   - If partial was at 1:1 → remaining targets **1:7**
   - If partial was at 1:2 → remaining targets **1:6**

**Alternative:** Some traders wait until **1:2** before adjusting stop to half.

### 4.3 Partial Profit Strategy

**Mandatory Partial ("si o si"):**
When key levels coincide (e.g., cycle 61 zone + resistance level), this is an obligatory partial. No debate.

> *"Esto es motivo de parcial, si o si."*

**Conservative Style (Many Partials):**
- Take partials at each significant Fibonacci level.
- **WARNING:** Too many partials destroy statistics. A 700-pip move with many partials yields far less than 700 pips of actual profit.

> *"En el fondo esos 700 pips no son 700 pips. Si, la posicion final llega a 700 pips, pero la cantidad de dinero que has hecho ahi con tantos parciales no me da estadisticamente para compensar el error."*

**Aggressive Style (Few Partials — Joaquin's personal approach for big targets):**
- Take ONE partial at the first key level (e.g., 61 zone).
- Let everything else ride to the final target.
- Result: Long losing/breakeven streaks, but when winners hit, they compensate massively.

**Rule:** Always evaluate your statistics. Do the numbers work with your partial approach? "Behind everything you do, there are numbers."

### 4.4 Super-Winner Approach

**Prerequisites:**
1. Master fixed-ratio management FIRST.
2. Accept that MOST super-winner attempts will fail.
3. Trade must already be secured (protective partial taken, remaining positions profitable).

**The Protocol:**
1. **At entry,** decide where the final exit target is (e.g., failure of a specific cycle, 200 extension).
2. Take protective partial first — this ensures the trade cannot lose money overall.
3. **LET IT RUN** to the predetermined exit. Do NOT close early from fear.
4. Price WILL make many erratic movements. Hard retracements, sideways, fake reversals. This is normal.
5. Maybe 10-20 per year actually reach the super-winner target.

> *"El secreto, al menos el que me funciona a mi: decidir donde vas a salir cuando entras."*
> *"Ya tomaste la decision cuando lo hiciste. Asi que no te eches para atras."*
> *"Si no lo intentas, nunca lo tienes."*

**Example from Joaquin:** "I decide those trades will exit at the failure of the blue cycle. If it doesn't reach there, to hell with it, let it take me out."

> *"Esas operaciones van a salir ahi. Ahi. Y si no llega, pues que me saque."*

**With fixed ratios, you will NEVER have a super-winning trade.** That is the trade-off.

> *"Si vas a un ratio fijo nunca tendras una operacion muy ganadora. Es mucho mas facil de aceptar psicologicamente. Pero en la vida, nunca vas a tener una operacion muy ganadora."*

### 4.5 The 10% Floating Profit Rule

When floating (unrealized) profit exceeds **10% of total account balance**, you MUST take some positions off to lock in gains. This is a hard risk management rule.

> *"Llevamos mas de un 10% siempre os digo en flotante que tenemos que coger beneficios."*

### 4.6 Failure Management Principle

**Managing failure (how you handle losses) is MORE IMPORTANT than accuracy percentage.**

> *"La gestion del fallo es mas importante que el porcentaje de acierto."*

**Protocol:**
- Accept that losses are inevitable and part of the business model.
- Cut losses early when price shows no reversal intent (Null Trade strategy).
- Never revenge trade after a loss.
- Track the QUALITY of your losses, not just their frequency.
- A string of well-managed losses is a sign of good trading, not bad trading.

> *"Yo no permito que me saque el stop completo."*
> ("I do not allow the stop to take me out completely.")

## 5. Risk Management

### 5.1 The 5 Profitability Factors

ALL five must be aligned. If one fails, you cannot be profitable.

| # | Factor | Description |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Discipline** (Disciplina) | Consistent execution of the plan. Tends to be stable once developed. |
| 2 | **Numbers** (Numeros) | Risk/reward ratios, statistics, lot sizing. Must mathematically work. |
| 3 | **Method** (Metodo) | Your reading + attack + management system. Should be proven. |
| 4 | **Assets** (Activos) | The instruments you trade. These CHANGE behavior over time. |
| 5 | **Personal Condition** (Condicion Personal) | Your availability, emotional state, life circumstances. Most volatile factor. |

> *"Si uno falla, no seras exitoso. Has conseguido crear una mezcla correcta de estos cinco factores."*
> *"Tu condicion personal no es la misma."*

### 5.2 When to Adapt

**Assets change:** What worked for 2-3 years may stop working. Find assets that suit your methodology AND your personal condition.

> *"Los activos que trabajas no se comportan igual. Te empiezas a dar cuenta de que los activos se mueven diferente."*

**Personal condition changes:** If your availability changed, timeframes may need to change. If emotional state changed, risk tolerance may be different.

**When an asset loses fractality:** If price becomes "random" and lacks coherent fractal structure, the method struggles. No specific quantitative threshold exists — this is a judgment call.

### 5.3 Business Plan Requirements

Without a business plan, you are gambling. The plan must define:
- Max loss per trade
- Profit target per trade
- Lot sizing formula (comes from "linea de trabajo" / line of work)
- Which assets to trade
- What timeframes
- Session schedule

## 6. Chart Reading Protocol

When analyzing a chart screenshot, follow this sequence:

### Step 1: Identify Points of Control
- Look for swing highs/lows with at least **2 closed candles** to the left and **2 closed candles** to the right.
- These are fractal peaks/troughs = candidate PoCs.
- **Validate** each PoC: Has price surpassed the fractal high by >= 1/3 of the retracement height?
- Mark validated PoCs. Discard invalidated ones (false breakouts).

### Step 2: Measure Impulses and Retracements
- From each validated PoC, measure the impulse (the movement that launched from the PoC).
- Apply Fibonacci retracement to the impulse.
- Check if price has retraced to **38.2%**.

### Step 3: Check 38.2% Activation
- If 38.2% has been touched → **Cycle is ACTIVATED.**
- If not → Wait. No cycle yet.

### Step 4: Mark Zones
For each activated cycle, mark:
- **High Zone** — above 61.8%
- **Low Zone** — below retracement area
- **38 level** — mid-reference
- **61.8 zone** — primary working zone
- **Break level** — where the cycle dies if exceeded by 1/3 of retracement

### Step 5: Check for Failures
- Examine movement origins. Did they reach the **25% minimum**?
- If origin is defective (didn't reach 25%) → **Failure projection active** to 161.8 or 200.
- Mark any active failure projections.

### Step 6: Look for Liquidity Grabs
- Identify swing lows/highs where stops have likely accumulated.
- Has price recently broken these levels and reversed? = Liquidity grab.
- These are entry signals if aligned with directional bias.

### Step 7: Check Cycle Evolution or Breaks
- Has any cycle's High/Low zone been entered?
- If entered: discard other zones. Monitor for break vs. evolution.
- Has a break been confirmed (1/3 rule)? → Cycle dead. Look for new PoCs.
- Has evolution occurred (larger movement + 38.2% retracement)? → Replace old cycle.

### Step 8: Check Retracement Completion
- For any active retracement, has price touched **130%**?
- If yes → Retracement complete, new impulse confirmed.
- If no → Retracement might still deepen. Remeasure if it deepens.

### Step 9: Identify Zone Confluences
- Do any zones from different cycles overlap at current price?
- Do Fibonacci levels from different measurements coincide?
- Confluences = highest conviction trading opportunities.

### Step 10: Determine Directional Bias
- Synthesize all the above into: "The reading model says price should go [direction] toward [level]."
- This becomes the basis for attack model decisions.

## 7. Common Situations & What To Do

### Situation 1: "Price is at the 61.8 zone in a downtrend cycle"
**Action:** This is a normal working zone. The 61.8 does NOT trigger evolution. Trade the zone per your direction bias. In a downtrend cycle, this is a sell zone. Enter short if confluence supports it.

### Situation 2: "Price entered the High Zone"
**Action:** IMMEDIATELY discard the 61 zone, 38 level, Low Zone, and Low Zone break level. Only the High Zone and its break level remain valid. Watch for: (a) price breaking through (cycle death), or (b) price reversing (potential evolution). In a downtrend cycle, this is where you look for sells.

### Situation 3: "Price entered the Low Zone in an uptrend cycle"
**Action:** This is a buy zone. Discard all other zones. Look for confluence with other cycle levels. If multiple 38 levels from different cycles coincide here, this is a mandatory buy ("si o si").

### Situation 4: "A cycle just broke (confirmed by 1/3 rule)"
**Action:** The cycle is dead. All its zones, levels, and marks are gone forever. Start looking for NEW Points of Control forming from the breakout movement. Monitor for new fractals at the next level. The breakout movement itself creates new reference points.

### Situation 5: "Price surpassed a level by less than 1/3 of retracement height"
**Action:** This is a FALSE BREAKOUT / MANIPULATION. Do NOT treat this as a valid break or a valid PoC. The institutional counterpart (market makers) triggered stops without genuine commitment. Wait for either a true break (>= 1/3) or a reversal.

### Situation 6: "The retracement hasn't touched 130% yet"
**Action:** The retracement is NOT complete. Do NOT assume the next impulse has begun. The price can still extend the retracement. If it deepens, remeasure your Fibonacci. Wait for the 130% touch.

### Situation 7: "A movement origin didn't reach 25% — failure activated"
**Action:** Failure projection is active. Price has projection to 161.8 or 200. For indices/commodities, favor the 200 target. For currencies, 161.8 is valid first target. Even if you're in a selling zone, you cannot ignore this long projection.

### Situation 8: "Multiple 38 levels from different cycles converge at the same price"
**Action:** This is an extremely powerful zone. Very difficult for price to move through easily in either direction. Price will likely bounce between the levels before making a powerful breakout. When it exits, it will move strongly. Be very vigilant about HOW it exits.

> *"Tenemos un 38, un 38, un 38, y un 38 aqui. Es decir, tenemos aqui una zona de 38 muy potente. Esto va a ser muy dificil tanto al alza como a la baja."*

### Situation 9: "Price is doing a liquidity grab in the direction opposite to my bias"
**Action:** If your reading model says price should go UP and price is breaking lows (grabbing liquidity), this is your entry signal. The cycle positioned you; the liquidity grab IS the attack. Enter long. Vice versa for shorts.

### Situation 10: "I have a very winning trade with floating profit above 10%"
**Action:** Secure some profits. Take positions off. This is a hard rule. The 10% floating profit rule exists to protect the account from giving back too much unrealized gain.

### Situation 11: "The trade went against me immediately"
**Action:** Consider exiting for a Null Trade (reduced loss). Do not let the stop be hit fully if price clearly has no intention of reversing. Active risk management means cutting early when appropriate. This is a sign of good failure management, not weakness.

> *"Yo no permito que me saque el stop completo."*

### Situation 12: "Price is between 161.8 and 200 in a large pattern"
**Action:** Expect failure-based progression. Price will NOT go straight to 200. It will hit 161.8, fail, retrace, hit new 161.8, fail again, working toward 200 through repeated failures. You can trade the internal cycles using Points of Control, or wait for the 200 level with a buffer for potential spikes.

### Situation 13: "I'm in a winning trade and scared of giving back profits"
**Action:** If you decided at entry to hold for a super-winner target, HOLD. You already made the decision. Don't back down because of fear. The erratic movements are normal. The trade is already profitable overall. The remaining positions are the "extra" — the attempt at the big move.

> *"No puedes achicarte. Ya tomaste la decision cuando lo hiciste."*

### Situation 14: "An old cycle evolved — new larger cycle formed"
**Action:** All old zones are DEAD. Mark new zones from the new, larger impulse. This creates bigger zones. You always want to work with the largest active fractal. All previous marks (zones, 38, 61, break levels) are replaced entirely.

### Situation 15: "There's a 61.8 retracement after confirmed failure of highs"
**Action:** ENTER. Any 61.8 after failure of highs is a reason to sell. Any 61.8 after failure of lows is a reason to buy. This is a standalone entry trigger.

### Situation 16: "I'm looking at a chart but don't see coherent fractality"
**Action:** The asset may be behaving too randomly for the method. Joaquin notes that assets can go through periods of lacking fractality. Consider switching to a different asset that shows more coherent fractal structure. There is no quantitative threshold — it's a judgment call based on whether you can identify clean PoCs, impulses, and retracements.

### Situation 17: "Should I take many small partials or one big one?"
**Action:** Evaluate your statistics. Too many partials dilute results and may not mathematically compensate for losses. One partial to secure the trade + letting the rest run yields bigger winners but requires more psychological tolerance. Both approaches work IF the numbers support them. Personal preference matters.

### Situation 18: "Price is oscillating in a zone, not breaking or reversing clearly"
**Action:** WAIT. Price is in the zone but hasn't decided. It will eventually either break through (cycle death) or reverse (potential evolution). Patience is required. Do not force entries in indecisive conditions.

### Situation 19: "I want to hold for a super-winner but haven't secured the trade yet"
**Action:** STOP. Take the protective partial first. The first partial's purpose is to secure the remaining positions. It ensures the trade won't cost money even if the rest fails. Only AFTER securing should you think about letting the remainder run.

### Situation 20: "Multiple timeframes show conflicting signals"
**Action:** This is a known gap in the documented method. Joaquin states the concept works the same on all timeframes ("no importa en cual estes, el concepto es el mismo"). General principle: larger timeframe cycles take priority over smaller ones. When in doubt, favor the higher timeframe reading.
